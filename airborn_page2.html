Designing VR in VR

I started prototyping the upcoming levels in VR today. I got the idea playing with Google's Tilt Brush the other day. I had been using pencil and paper for months now, mocking up level and model ideas. Keep in mind I am not good at drawing in the first place, so drawing something meant to be in 3D in 2 dimensions was borderline impossible for me. After a good 5 minutes in Tilt Brush, I realized I had stumbled on a truly promethean tool. Tilt Brush is basically the traditional pencil on steroids. Also, a recent update to Tilt Brush where they added the ability to scale your 3D drawings on a whim truly makes it shine. Using this scaling mechanism, I am able to design a level from above, then scale the level bigger and bigger until I am literally inside it. This allows me to see what the level would look like from the player's perspective inside the game, before even modelling anything!




Yellow lines are paths




Red marks are enemies


I am uncontainably stoked to have this tool for prototyping because it helps me bridge the gap from my mind to the computer screen much quicker than I thought was even possible. I swear, every time I put on the Vive HMD I am blown away from simply glimpsing how much VR is going to change the world. I can't wait for future Tilt Brush updates because this software is absolutely changing the game.


Anyway, back to Airborn. now that I have this kind of power at my fingertips I'll be utilizing it to design the rest of the levels over the next few days.

Just Photoshop Things

So I got this sweet digital drawing tablet so I could start hand drawing textures for Airborn and it's already one of my favorite gadgets! Keep in mind I do not consider myself an artist but this thing lets me do things I never thought I could do. The tool truly allows you to draw in a way you never could with a pencil. First of all, I'm left handed, so drawing with the mouse has always been difficult for me. Second, this tool has programmable buttons on it, one of which defaults to the undo command which is unbelievably useful! I can draw a line a hundred times a minute, and if I don't like it I just press a single button with my right hand and draw it again. My draw actions per minute must have skyrocketed and I'm so glad I bought this tool!

I also took some time to start designing some branding and logo ideas for the game. I think I like one of the pictures so much I'll use it for the main store image and definitely use it as the background for this blog.

Art Is Not My Forte

Slowly but surely I am learning how to use Blender and Photoshop to design things I need for the game. Finding an artist for graphics has always been a great challenge for me, so I've finally decided to do it all myself. I never realized there was so much involved in 3D graphic design for games. I figured you make a sightly 3D mesh, drag and drop it into Unity, and you're set. Turns out if you really want something to look right and perform optimally there are a lot more things to think about. I've slowly been catching myself up on 3D modelling, texturing, UV wrapping, normal mapping, LOD groups, rigging and animating. Despite having a lot on my plate, I am having a blast!




My very first creature animation. Modelled, rigged, animated, loaded into Unity and promptly exploded. The circle of life.
